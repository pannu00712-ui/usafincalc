💰 What Is Property Tax and How Is It Calculated?

Property tax is an ad valorem tax — meaning "according to value" — levied on real estate by local governments. It's the primary funding source for schools, roads, police, fire departments, and other local services.

The Basic Formula

Property Tax = (Assessed Value × Assessment Ratio - Exemptions) × Mill Rate / 1,000

  • Assessed Value: The value assigned to your property by the local assessor (often a percentage of market value).
  • Assessment Ratio: The percentage of market value that is taxed. Some states use 100% (full value), others use 50% (Michigan) or 35% (Ohio).
  • Exemptions: Reductions in assessed value for homesteads, seniors, veterans, or disabled homeowners.
  • Mill Rate: The tax rate expressed in mills (1 mill = $1 per $1,000 of assessed value). A 20 mill rate = 2.0%.

📝 Example Calculation

$300,000 home in Ohio

Step 1: Assessed value = $300,000 × 35% = $105,000
Step 2: Apply homestead exemption ($25,000) = $80,000 taxable
Step 3: Tax = $80,000 × 20 mills / 1,000 = $1,600 (before rollback credits)
Step 4: Apply 12.5% rollback credit = $1,400 final tax

🏠 Common Property Tax Exemptions

Most states offer exemptions that reduce your taxable assessed value. Here are the most common:

🏡

Homestead Exemption

Reduces assessed value for owner-occupied primary residences. Varies from $2,000 (GA) to $50,000 (FL).
👴

Senior Exemption

Additional exemption for homeowners 65+. Income limits often apply. Up to $65,000 (NY Enhanced STAR).
🎖️

Veterans Exemption

For honorably discharged or disabled veterans. Can be full exemption for 100% disabled veterans.
♿

Disabled Exemption

For permanently disabled homeowners. Often combined with senior exemption programs.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between assessed value and market value?
Market value is what your home would sell for on the open market. Assessed value is the value assigned by your local assessor for tax purposes. Some states assess at 100% of market value, others use a fraction (e.g., 50% in Michigan, 35% in Ohio).
What is a mill rate?
A mill is $1 of tax per $1,000 of assessed value. So a 20 mill rate = 2.0% tax rate. To convert mills to percentage, divide by 10 (20 mills ÷ 10 = 2.0%).
How can I reduce my property tax bill?
1. Apply for all available exemptions (homestead, senior, veteran, disabled). 2. Appeal your assessment if you believe it's too high. 3. Check for errors in your property's description. 4. In some states, consider tax deferral programs for seniors.
Which state has the highest property tax rate?
New Jersey has the highest average effective property tax rate at 2.23%, followed by Illinois at 2.08% and New Hampshire at 1.99%.
Which state has no property tax?
No state has zero property tax. Property taxes are the primary funding source for local governments. However, some states have very low rates (Hawaii: 0.28%, Alabama: 0.41%).
What is a homestead exemption?
A homestead exemption reduces the assessed value of your primary residence for tax purposes. The amount varies by state. For example, Florida offers $50,000 off assessed value, while Georgia offers $2,000–$20,000 depending on the county.
How often are properties reassessed?
Reassessment frequency varies by state and county. Some states reassess annually (California, Colorado), others every 3–5 years (many Midwest states), and some only at sale (Prop 13 in California).
