Arizona Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 0.80%–1.20% County + City + School

Assessment Ratios:

  • Primary Residence: 10% of full cash value
  • Commercial/Investment: 16.5% of full cash value
  • Agricultural: 16.5% of full cash value

Key Programs:

  • Homeowner Exemption: $2,500 reduction in assessed value for primary residences
  • Senior Property Valuation Protection: Freezes assessed value for seniors 65+ (income-based)
  • Disabled Veterans Exemption: Up to $3,000 reduction for eligible disabled veterans
  • Agricultural Land: Special valuation for farm/ranch land

Tax Composition: County ~35%, City ~25%, School ~30%, Special districts ~10%

Source: Arizona Department of Revenue — azdor.gov · Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Arizona property tax assessment ratio?
Arizona uses different assessment ratios: 10% for primary residences and 16.5% for commercial/other properties. For example, a $400,000 primary home has an assessed value of $40,000.
What is the Homeowner Exemption in Arizona?
The Homeowner Exemption provides a $2,500 reduction in assessed value for owner-occupied primary residences. This saves approximately $30–$50 per year depending on the tax rate.
