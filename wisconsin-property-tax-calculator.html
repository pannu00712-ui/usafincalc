Wisconsin Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 1.5%–2.0% County + City + School

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 100% of market value.

Key Programs:

  • Homestead Credit: State-paid credit for low to moderate income homeowners
  • Lottery Credit: Credit from state lottery revenue for primary residences
  • Senior Exemption: 65+ homeowners may qualify for additional exemptions
  • Disabled Veterans Exemption: Available for qualifying disabled veterans

Tax Composition: School ~50%, County ~20%, City ~20%, Special districts ~10%

Source: Wisconsin Department of Revenue — revenue.wi.gov · Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Wisconsin property tax rate?
Wisconsin's average effective rate is approximately 1.5%–2.0%. Milwaukee County has the highest rates while rural counties have lower rates.
What is the Lottery Credit in Wisconsin?
The Lottery Credit is a state-funded credit that reduces property taxes for primary residences. The amount varies by municipality and is funded by state lottery revenue.
