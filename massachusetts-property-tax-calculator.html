Massachusetts Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 1.05%–1.40% City + School + County

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 100% of market value.

Key Programs:

  • Residential Exemption: Many cities offer exemption for owner-occupied primary residences (varies by city)
  • Senior Circuit Breaker: Tax credit for seniors 65+ (up to $2,000, income-based)
  • Proposition 2½: Limits tax levy increases to 2.5% plus new growth
  • Veterans Exemption: Available for eligible veterans

Tax Composition: City ~30%, School ~60%, County ~5%, Special districts ~5%

Source: Massachusetts Department of Revenue — Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Massachusetts property tax rate?
Massachusetts property tax rates vary by city. The average effective rate is approximately 1.05%–1.40%. Boston has a rate around 1.0%, while some smaller cities can exceed 2.0%.
What is the Senior Circuit Breaker in Massachusetts?
The Senior Circuit Breaker is a refundable tax credit for seniors 65+ whose property tax exceeds 10% of their income. The credit can be up to $2,000, depending on income and tax amount.
