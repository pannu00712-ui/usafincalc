Georgia Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 1.0%–1.2% (below national average) County + City + School

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 40% of market value.

Key Exemptions:

  • Homestead Exemption: Varies by county, typically $2,000–$20,000 reduction
  • Senior Exemption: Additional exemption for homeowners 65+ (varies by county)
  • School Tax Exemption: Seniors 65+ may be exempt from school taxes in many counties
  • Disabled Veterans: Up to $75,000 exemption

Tax Composition: School districts ~50%, County ~30%, Municipal ~15%, Special districts ~5%

Source: Georgia Department of Revenue — Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Georgia property tax rate?
Georgia's average effective rate is approximately 1.0%–1.2%, which is below the national average. Rates vary by county and city.
What is the Homestead Exemption in Georgia?
The Homestead Exemption reduces the assessed value of your primary residence. The amount varies by county, typically from $2,000 to $20,000. Some counties also offer additional exemptions for seniors and disabled veterans.
