Known Calculator Limitations

A few things our calculators don't account for that could affect your actual numbers:

  • Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) in complex income situations
  • Retirement projections assume consistent returns — actual markets vary significantly
  • Mortgage calculators may not capture all lender fees, PMI variations, or escrow adjustments
  • State tax calculations may not include all municipal or local taxes
  • Self-employment calculators assume standard deduction — your itemized deductions may be higher

Think of our numbers as a starting point — useful for planning conversations with professionals, not as final figures you'd act on directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use USA FinCalc results on my tax return?

No. Our calculators provide estimates for planning purposes only. For official tax filing, use IRS-certified tax software or a licensed CPA.

How accurate are the tax bracket calculations?

Our brackets are sourced directly from IRS publications and are updated every January. For mid-year calculations, we note the applicable tax year prominently on each calculator page.

Does the mortgage calculator include all costs?

Our mortgage calculator estimates principal + interest + estimated property tax + insurance (PITI). It does not include all possible lender fees, HOA dues, or specific local taxes. Always get a full Loan Estimate from your lender.
