Michigan Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 1.20%–1.80% County + City + School

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 50% of market value (State Equalized Value).

Key Programs:

  • Homestead Exemption: Exempts up to 18 mills for owner-occupied primary residences
  • Proposal A (1994): Caps annual assessment increases at 5% or inflation (whichever is lower)
  • Senior/Disabled Exemption: Additional exemption for qualifying seniors and disabled individuals
  • Veterans Exemption: Available for disabled veterans

Tax Composition: School ~45%, County ~20%, City ~20%, Special districts ~15%

Source: Michigan Department of Treasury — Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Michigan property tax rate?
Michigan's average effective rate is approximately 1.20%–1.80%. Rates vary by county and city, with the homestead exemption providing significant savings for primary residences.
What is the Homestead Exemption in Michigan?
The Homestead Exemption exempts up to 18 mills of property tax for owner-occupied primary residences. This can save homeowners hundreds to thousands of dollars per year compared to non-homestead properties.
