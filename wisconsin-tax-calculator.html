About Wisconsin income tax

Wisconsin uses a progressive state income tax from 3.5% to 7.65%, applied alongside federal income tax, Social Security, and Medicare withholdings.

Wisconsin cities and counties do not levy a separate local income tax. Wisconsin uses a four-bracket progressive system, and the top bracket only applies to income above roughly $332,720 for single filers.

Frequently asked questions

Does Wisconsin have a state income tax?
Yes. Wisconsin has progressive state income tax from 3.5% to 7.65%. The effective rate after deductions is typically somewhat lower than the top bracket rate.
How much is taken out of my paycheck in Wisconsin?
On a $75,000 salary in Wisconsin, deductions include federal income tax ($7,670), Social Security ($4,650), Medicare ($1,088), and state income tax ($2,632). Take-home: ~$58,961/year.
How progressive is Wisconsin's income tax really?
Quite progressive at the bottom and fairly flat in the middle. Wisconsin's first bracket (3.5%) covers income up to about $15,110, then jumps to 4.4% for a wide middle range, 5.3% for upper-middle income, and 7.65% only above roughly $332,720. Most full-time workers pay an effective rate well below the top bracket.
