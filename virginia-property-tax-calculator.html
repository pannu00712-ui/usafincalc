Virginia Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 0.80%–1.10% County + City

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 100% of market value.

Key Relief Programs:

  • Senior/Disabled Tax Relief: Local option for residents 65+ or permanently disabled (partial or full relief based on income)
  • Veterans Exemption: 100% disabled veterans may be fully exempt
  • Vehicle Personal Property Tax: Separate tax on vehicles (not included in this calculator)
  • Land Use Tax: Deferred valuation for agricultural/forestry land

Tax Composition: County/City ~100% (no separate school tax in VA)

Source: Virginia Department of Taxation — Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Virginia property tax rate?
Virginia's average effective rate is approximately 0.80%–1.10%. Rates vary by county and city, with Northern Virginia generally having higher rates and lower home values in rural areas.
What is the senior tax relief in Virginia?
Many Virginia counties and cities offer real estate tax relief for seniors 65+ and permanently disabled residents. Relief can be partial (50%) or full (100%) based on income and asset limits. Check with your local commissioner of revenue.
