Tennessee Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 0.60%–0.80% (one of the lowest in the US) County + City

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 25% of market value.

Key Programs:

  • Homestead Exemption: $7,500 reduction in assessed value for primary residences
  • Senior Tax Freeze (65+): Freezes property tax at the base year amount — eligible seniors pay the same tax amount regardless of rate increases
  • Disabled Veterans Exemption: Exemption for 100% disabled veterans
  • No State Income Tax: Tennessee does not tax wages

Tax Composition: County ~50%, City ~30%, School ~20%

Source: Tennessee Department of Revenue — tn.gov/revenue · Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Tennessee property tax rate?
Tennessee's average effective rate is approximately 0.60%–0.80%, among the lowest in the United States. Rates vary by county and city.
What is the Homestead Exemption in Tennessee?
The Homestead Exemption provides a $7,500 reduction in assessed value for owner-occupied primary residences. This saves approximately $50–$80 per year.
How does the Senior Tax Freeze work in Tennessee?
The Senior Tax Freeze program allows homeowners 65+ to freeze their property tax amount at the base year. Even if tax rates increase, your tax bill stays at the frozen amount. Homestead and senior freeze work together — the freeze applies to the homestead-adjusted amount.
