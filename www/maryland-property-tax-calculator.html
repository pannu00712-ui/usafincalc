Maryland Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 0.90%–1.50% State + County + Municipal

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 100% of market value.

Key Programs:

  • Homestead Exemption: Limits annual assessment increases to 10% for primary residences
  • Homeowner's Tax Credit: Income-based credit for eligible homeowners
  • Senior Tax Credit: Credit for homeowners 65+ with income under certain limits
  • Disabled Veterans Exemption: Exemption for 100% disabled veterans

Tax Composition: State ~25%, County ~35%, Municipal ~20%, School ~20%

Source: Maryland Department of Assessments and Taxation — dat.maryland.gov · Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Maryland property tax rate?
Maryland's average effective rate is approximately 0.90%–1.50%. The state has both state (0.112%) and county taxes combined. Montgomery and Howard counties have some of the highest rates.
What is the Homestead Exemption in Maryland?
The Homestead Exemption limits annual assessment increases to 10% for owner-occupied primary residences. This protects homeowners from sudden tax spikes due to rising property values.
