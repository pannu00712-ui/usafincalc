Colorado Property Tax Information 2026

Assessment Rate: 6.95% for residential property (down from 7.15% in 2025) Property Tax

Mill Rate Range: 40–85 mills (4%–8.5% on assessed value) depending on county and district.

Key Programs:

  • Senior Exemption: 50% exemption on first $200,000 of actual value for 65+ (varies by county)
  • TABOR (1992): Limits annual tax revenue growth to inflation + population growth
  • Gallagher Amendment Repeal (2020): Assessment rates now set by legislature
  • Disabled Veterans Exemption: Up to 100% exemption for eligible disabled veterans

Tax Composition: County ~30%, City ~20%, School ~40%, Special districts ~10%

Source: Colorado Department of Local Affairs — cdola.colorado.gov · Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Colorado property tax assessment rate for 2026?
The residential assessment rate for 2026 is 6.95%. This is the percentage of your home's market value that is subject to taxation. For example, a $500,000 home has an assessed value of $34,750.
What is the Senior Exemption in Colorado?
Colorado offers a property tax exemption for seniors 65+ that exempts 50% of the first $200,000 of actual value. This can save seniors up to $500–$700 per year depending on the mill rate.
