About South Carolina income tax

South Carolina uses a progressive state income tax from 0% to 6%, applied alongside federal income tax, Social Security, and Medicare withholdings.

South Carolina cities and counties do not levy a separate local income tax. The state's top marginal rate (6%) kicks in at a relatively low income level, so most full-time workers' effective rate sits close to, but below, that top rate.

Frequently asked questions

Does South Carolina have a state income tax?
Yes. South Carolina has progressive state income tax from 0% to 6%. The effective rate after deductions is typically somewhat lower than the top bracket rate.
How much is taken out of my paycheck in South Carolina?
On a $75,000 salary in South Carolina, deductions include federal income tax ($7,670), Social Security ($4,650), Medicare ($1,088), and state income tax ($3,343). Take-home: ~$58,250/year.
How does South Carolina's $0 bracket work?
South Carolina taxes the first roughly $3,640 of taxable income at 0%, then 3% up to about $18,230, then 6% above that. Because the brackets are relatively narrow, most full-time workers earning a typical salary end up with most of their income falling in the 6% bracket.
