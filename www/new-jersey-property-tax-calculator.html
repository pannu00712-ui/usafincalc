New Jersey Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 2.23% (highest in the United States) #1 in the Nation

Homestead Rebate (2026): Up to $1,500 for eligible homeowners with income under $250,000. Amount varies by income and property tax bill.

Senior Freeze (Property Tax Reimbursement): Reimburses eligible seniors 65+ for property tax increases. Income limit: $110,000 (married) / $87,000 (single).

Key Exemptions:

  • Veterans Exemption: Up to $250 reduction in annual taxes for honorably discharged veterans
  • Senior Citizen Exemption: $250 reduction for seniors 65+ with income under $30,000
  • Disabled Persons Exemption: $250 reduction for permanently disabled individuals
  • Surviving Spouse Exemption: $250 reduction for widows/widowers

Property Tax Composition: Municipal (local government) ~30%, School District ~50%, County ~15%, Special Districts ~5%

Assessment Ratio: Most counties assess at 100% of market value. Some municipalities use a ratio (e.g., 50% in some cities).

Source: New Jersey Division of Taxation — nj.gov/treasury/taxation · Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions — New Jersey Property Tax

What is the New Jersey property tax rate for 2026?
New Jersey has the highest property taxes in the United States with an average effective rate of 2.23%. County rates range from 1.0% in Salem to over 3.0% in Essex and Passaic counties. The total rate includes municipal, school, county, and special district taxes.
What is the Homestead Rebate in New Jersey?
The Homestead Rebate is an annual property tax credit for eligible homeowners. For 2026, the maximum rebate is $1,500 for homeowners with income under $250,000. The actual rebate amount is based on your income and property tax bill.
How is New Jersey property tax calculated?
Property tax is calculated as:
Tax = (Assessed Value × Effective Tax Rate) - Exemptions - Rebates
Example: $450,000 home in Bergen County (2.2% rate) with $1,000 rebate: ($450,000 × 0.022) - $1,000 = $8,900 annual tax.
What is the Senior Freeze program in New Jersey?
The Senior Freeze (Property Tax Reimbursement) program reimburses eligible seniors 65+ for property tax increases from one year to the next. Income limits for 2026: $110,000 for married couples and $87,000 for single filers.
Which New Jersey county has the highest property taxes?
Essex, Passaic, and Union counties typically have the highest effective tax rates, often exceeding 2.5%–3.0%. Bergen, Morris, and Somerset have moderate rates around 2.0%–2.3%. Salem and Cumberland have the lowest rates around 1.0%–1.5%.
What is the veterans property tax exemption in New Jersey?
Honorably discharged New Jersey veterans may be eligible for a $250 reduction in annual property taxes. Disabled veterans may qualify for a full property tax exemption depending on the severity of their disability.
How often are New Jersey properties reassessed?
Most New Jersey counties conduct annual reassessments of all properties. However, some municipalities use a 4–5 year reassessment cycle. If your assessment is too high, you can appeal to your county Board of Taxation.
When are New Jersey property taxes due?
Property taxes are typically due quarterly: February 1, May 1, August 1, and November 1. Most municipalities offer a 2% discount for full payment by the first quarter deadline.
Can I deduct New Jersey property taxes on my federal return?
Yes. New Jersey property tax is deductible on your federal income tax return if you itemize. However, the SALT (State And Local Tax) deduction is capped at $10,000 ($5,000 for married filing separately) for the 2026 tax year.
Why are property taxes so high in New Jersey?
New Jersey property taxes are high due to several factors: high cost of education (school taxes account for ~50% of the bill), home rule (566 municipalities with their own governments), limited state aid, and high local service costs (police, fire, infrastructure).
