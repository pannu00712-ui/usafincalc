Minnesota Property Tax Information 2026

Average Effective Rate: 1.0%–1.3% County + City + School

Assessment Ratio: Residential property assessed at 100% of market value.

Key Programs:

  • Homestead Credit (2026): 20% of tax bill up to $400 maximum for primary residences
  • Senior Deferral Program: Allows seniors 65+ to defer 50% or 100% of property tax payments (income-based)
  • Disabled Veterans Exemption: Available for qualifying disabled veterans
  • Agricultural Land: Special valuation for farm property

Tax Composition: County ~25%, City ~25%, School ~45%, Special districts ~5%

Source: Minnesota Department of Revenue — revenue.state.mn.us · Updated July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Minnesota property tax rate?
Minnesota's average effective rate is approximately 1.0%–1.3%. Rates vary by county and city, with Hennepin and Ramsey counties having some of the highest rates.
What is the Homestead Credit in Minnesota for 2026?
The Homestead Credit is a state-paid credit that reduces property taxes for primary residences. For 2026, the credit is 20% of your property tax up to a maximum of $400. You must apply with your county assessor.
How does the Senior Deferral Program work in Minnesota?
The Senior Deferral Program allows homeowners 65+ to defer up to 100% of their property tax payments. The deferred amount becomes a lien on the property with simple interest (currently 3%). Income limits apply — check with the Minnesota Department of Revenue for current thresholds.
